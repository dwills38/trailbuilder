📚 Guides

Plain-language buyer's and compatibility guides for the enthusiast home mechanic — how mountain-bike standards fit together, the real-world trade-offs, and how the builder checks each one — plus a growing set of riding-skills guides covering body position, braking, cornering, and terrain features. Brand-neutral and free of affiliate links.

Compatibility notes on this page describe what BuildMyMTB checks and are grounded in the tool's real rules. Specs vary by model and year — always confirm against the manufacturer before you buy. This guide contains no affiliate links.

This is general coaching guidance distilled from cited sources, not a substitute for in-person coaching that can watch your form in real time — the felt, real-time layer of any physical skill can't be transferred by a page. Where a maneuver carries real injury risk, ride within your comfort level and consider time with a local coach or a maintained progression feature before pushing further. This guide contains no affiliate links.

About this prototype. Pick a whole groupset / wheelset / brake set / cockpit kit (green dashed cards) to drop in all the parts at once — that group is then priced as the bundle; swap any single part and it reverts to per-part pricing. Every card shows a compatibility dot (green = no conflicts found among the dimensions we check — some makers publish no spec data, so green isn’t a guarantee, yellow = check the warning first, red won’t fit, grey = nothing selected yet) and a sample weight, and the catalog is sorted compatible-first (use the Sort menu to reorder by name, price or weight). Specs, prices and weights are illustrative samples unless a part shows a green ✓ Verified badge (checked against the manufacturer, with a source link). Logic lives in src/compat.js; run node validate.js to check the data and npm test for the suite. Mullet (29 front / 27.5 rear) builds work on mullet-capable frames — try the Mullet build demo or pick a 27.5 rear wheel + tire on the YT Capra.
