## Scope scaffold (what each discipline adds on top of L1/L2)

**DSC-1 — Discipline craft = core skills applied under a discipline's dominant constraint.** The
four core-skill chapters (fundamentals, braking-traction, cornering, climbing-descending) and the
L2 features chapter are the substrate; each discipline *reprioritizes* them:

- **XC (cross-country)** — dominant constraint: **efficiency over a long effort.** Seated-power
  climbing economy, race-line efficiency, smooth low-energy technique, remounts, pacing. The
  descending/features skills matter but the defining craft is *not wasting energy*. *(cues: L3 gap
  — DSC-2)*
- **Enduro** — dominant constraint: **timed descents with untimed transitions, over a long day.**
  Stage/transition management, line memory (learning a stage), pacing across stages, technique when
  **fatigued**, kit/tyre-pressure choices for mixed stages. Applies the full descending + features
  ladder at race intensity. *(cues: L3 gap — DSC-3)*
- **DH (downhill) race technique** — dominant constraint: **maximum speed on committed terrain.**
  High-speed line choice, **braking-point discipline** (where NOT to brake), terrain-reading at
  speed, holding position under big loads. The committed end of the descending/features ladders.
  *(cues: L3 gap — DSC-4)*
- **DJ / pump-track / park** — dominant constraint: **airtime and transitions, not distance.**
  Pump-track flow (pumping, no pedalling), airtime control, whip/trick foundations, transition
  reading (takeoff/landing shapes). Built on the L2 pumping/jump ladder (`terrain-features.md`).
  *(cues: L3 gap — DSC-5)*

- *Tier:* B scaffold (the scope and the "L1/L2 applied under constraint" framing). Source: the L1
  chapters + `terrain-features.md` for the substrate; discipline-specific cues unfetched.

**DSC-6 — The biomechanics / sports-science "why" layer belongs here (Tier-C).** Per the curriculum,
L3 is where the *mechanism* under the cues gets grounded in research: braking dynamics and weight
transfer (grounds BRK-1/5), centre-of-mass management (grounds FND-4, CLD-5's "flexion creates
extension"), traction physics, and vision/perception under load. Highest rigor for *why*, thinnest
MTB-specific coverage — a dedicated Tier-C pass (PubMed/Exa) is a clean, non-walled future round.
*(fetches: L3 gap — DSC-7)*
- *Tier:* C target (none fetched yet). Source: named as a gap; reachable via bio-research/PubMed MCP.

## PROGRESSIONS (the organizing principle)

### The ladder (what depends on what)
- **No discipline craft before solid L1 + the relevant L2 rungs.** DH/park require the L2 features
  ladder (drops, jumps, steeps) intact; XC/enduro require solid climbing, braking, and cornering.
  A rider is not "doing DH technique" if the base is shaky — they're at risk. Diagnose bottom-up
  (CCH-1).
- **Gear escalates hard here:** DH and park routinely mean full-face, knee/elbow, sometimes
  neck/body protection — the discipline's norms are part of the coaching content, not an aside.

### How this chapter couples
- XC ↔ climbing-descending (climbing economy) + braking (efficiency).
- Enduro ↔ the whole corpus at race intensity + fatigue-state technique.
- DH ↔ descending + features at the committed end.
- DJ/park ↔ `terrain-features.md` pumping/jump ladder.
- DSC-6 (biomechanics) ↔ grounds facts across *all* chapters — a cross-cutting deepening.
