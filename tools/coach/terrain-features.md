# Terrain Features — Coach Corpus

**Maturity: skeleton** (L2 — the priority target for the next training round). Pumping · wheel
lifts · manuals · bunny hops · drops · jumps · rock gardens & roots · switchbacks · steeps &
chutes. Read [`INDEX.md`](INDEX.md) first (corpus rules, tiers, and especially the **SAFETY-FIRST
PROGRESSION** rule — it bites hardest in this chapter).

Fact IDs are stable and append-only (`TER-n`). **This chapter is deliberately a scaffold, not a
set of how-to cues yet.** Per corpus rule 2, technique cues require fetched sources; the bootstrap
ingest targeted L1, so the *individual maneuver cues here are not yet fetched* and are marked as
gaps. What IS laid down now is the **safety ladder** — the prerequisite order the next round must
respect — because getting the *order* wrong is how a features chapter injures people. Do not add a
maneuver's cues without also adding its prerequisites, gear/terrain envelope, and error-correction
(rule 4).

## The features ladder (the scaffold — build in this order, never skip rungs)

**TER-1 — The L2 maneuver ladder, lowest rung first.** These maneuvers are *not* independent; each
depends on the ones beneath it (and on solid L1 fundamentals — body position, braking, vision).
The safe teaching order (per the IDEA/prerequisite principle, `coaching-methodology.md` CCH-3 and
the SAFETY-FIRST rule):

1. **Pumping** — generating/absorbing energy from terrain without pedalling. Prereq: FND ready
   position, heavy-feet. The base of all "using the terrain" skills. *(cues: L2 gap — TER-2)*
2. **Wheel lifts** — front-wheel lift, then rear-wheel lift, on demand. Prereq: pumping, FND
   balance. The rung that unlocks manuals, bunny hops, and drops. *(cues: L2 gap — TER-3)*
3. **Manual** — rolling on the rear wheel without pedalling (balance point). Prereq: front-wheel
   lift, FND-4 balance. *(cues: L2 gap — TER-4)*
4. **Bunny hop** — lifting both wheels over an obstacle. Prereq: front + rear wheel lifts.
   *(cues: L2 gap — TER-5)*
5. **Rolling drops → small committed drops** — riding off a ledge, wheels-together, controlled.
   Prereq: wheel lifts / manual (to manage the front on landing), committed body position,
   braking control. *(cues: L2 gap — TER-6)*
6. **Jumps (tabletop → gap)** — leaving the ground on a takeoff, controlled in the air and on
   landing. Prereq: pumping, bunny hop, drops, committed position. **The rung most dangerous to
   teach out of order.** *(cues: L2 gap — TER-7)*

Alongside this vertical ladder sit the **terrain-reading** features that are advanced *applications*
of L1 skills rather than new airborne maneuvers:
- **Rock gardens & roots** — L1 body position + vision + line choice at speed on chattery terrain.
  Prereq: solid ready position (FND-1/2), heavy-feet (FND-5), surface reading (BRK-7). *(TER-8 gap)*
- **Switchbacks** — tight, often steep, often off-camber corners. Prereq: off-camber cornering
  (COR-8) + steep-descent technique (CLD-5). *(TER-9 — partial seed below)*
- **Steeps & chutes** — the committed end of the descending ladder. Prereq: CLD-5 steep roll.
  *(TER-10 — cross-ref seed below)*

- *Tier:* B-consensus scaffold (the *ordering* and prerequisites, per the IDEA/prerequisite
  principle and standard coaching progression). Source: `coaching-methodology.md` CCH-3 (IDEA),
  CCH-8 (progression design); the L1 chapters for the named prerequisites. **The individual rung
  cues are unfetched — see Gaps.**

## Partial seeds (cross-referenced from L1 chapters)

**TER-9 — Switchbacks = off-camber cornering (COR-8) meets steep-descent technique (CLD-5).** A
switchback is a very tight, frequently steep and off-camber turn. The corpus already holds its two
parent skills: off-camber cornering (COR-8 — smooth inputs, no sudden braking, outside foot
weighted, "ride it or don't") and gradient-scaled steep positioning (CLD-5). The dedicated
switchback technique (tight pivot vs wide line, foot-out, front-wheel placement, drifting the rear)
is **not yet fetched.**
- *Prereq:* COR-8, CLD-5.
- *Gear:* helmet, dropper; protection scaled to consequence (switchbacks often have exposure).
- *Progression:* solid off-camber corners + steep rolls *before* linking them into a switchback.
- *Tier:* B (the composition is sound; the dedicated cues are an L2 gap). Source: cross-ref COR-8,
  CLD-5.

**TER-10 — Steeps & chutes are the committed rung of the descending ladder (CLD-5).** The steep
roll → longer steep → consequential chute progression lives across this chapter and
`climbing-descending.md` CLD-5. Gear escalates with consequence (full-face + more protection on
real steeps), and the fear-response management (evo/Fluidride — move forward at the knees, relax,
don't retreat rearward) is the key coaching content. Dedicated drop-in / chute-entry technique is
**not yet fetched.**
- *Prereq:* CLD-5 (steep roll), BRK-3 (threshold braking).
- *Tier:* B-consensus for what's cross-referenced; committed-chute cues are an L2 gap. Source: CLD-5.

## PROGRESSIONS (the organizing principle)

### The ladder (what depends on what) — the whole point of this chapter
- **Everything here sits on solid L1.** A rider whose body position, braking, or vision is shaky is
  **not ready** for any L2 feature — the coach fixes the base first (CCH-1 pyramid, diagnose
  bottom-up).
- **The vertical ladder (TER-1) is strict:** pumping → wheel lifts → manual/bunny hop → drops →
  jumps. **Skipping rungs is the defining safety failure of a features chapter.** A rider cannot
  safely control a drop landing without a front-wheel lift; cannot safely jump without a bunny hop
  and drop control. The next round MUST preserve this order.
- **Gear escalates with the rung:** helmet always; knee pads as drops/jumps/steeps enter; full-face
  and more protection for committed jumps, real steeps, and park/DH features (cross-ref
  `discipline-craft.md`).

### How this chapter couples
- Pumping → cornering (pumping berms) and jumping (takeoff timing).
- Wheel lifts/manual → drops (front management on landing) and technical climbing (step-ups).
- Steeps/switchbacks → the descending and cornering chapters (their parent skills).

## Gaps (this chapter is mostly gap by design — it is the next round's target)
- **Every maneuver's cues are unfetched (TER-2..TER-8):** pumping, front/rear wheel lifts, manual,
  bunny hop, drops, jumps, rock gardens/roots. Each needs a fetched Tier-A/B source, the full
  introduce→develop→commit progression, prerequisites, gear/terrain envelope, and error-correction
  — per rule 4. **This is the #1 priority next round.**
- **Jumps need the most careful treatment:** tabletop-before-gap, the "never case it / never dead-
  sailor" safety content, and the go/no-go criteria — this is the highest-injury-risk content in
  the whole corpus and must be laddered impeccably, not just described.
- **Drops need explicit consequence-scaling** (roll-off → 1-foot → progressively bigger) with the
  "match the landing" and speed-not-hesitation content, sourced.
- **Switchback and committed-chute cues (TER-9/TER-10)** are cross-referenced but not dedicated —
  L2 gap.
- **No certification-body L2 skill list** (walled) to grade this chapter against — L4 gap.
