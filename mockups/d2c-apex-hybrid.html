The shop behind the tools

D-Dubs Works is an independent maker. No manufacturer pays for placement, sort order, or a favorable verdict — the tools exist so a rider can know, before spending a dollar, whether the parts they want actually fit. Affiliate links may fund the site; they never bias it, and every one is labeled.

The flagship's promise, kept family-wide: “BuildMyMTB is a free, unbiased bike-building tool with accurate data — pick parts and see, in real time, whether they fit together, plus running price and weight.”

Straight to the data

How D2c coexists with the approved D1 + D4 (it's an addition, not a replacement):
  • Why it exists: D2a and D2b are two tones for the same URL — the only apex currently owned is ddubsworks.com. Whichever tone is picked, the other job still lands on that domain. One page doing both (doors above the fold, maker below) means one page to maintain instead of choosing what to turn away.
  • With D1 (family switcher): untouched — D1 keeps serving cross-hops inside the product sites; no product domain gains any landing page.
  • Beyond D4 (/home.html): everything D2a and D2b each add that D4 can't — the canonical company face for partners and the bike-type-agnostic front door — without a second apex page. D4 stays the deep per-product story; this page links to it rather than repeating it.
  • Cost: small — one static page plus pointing the apex domain at it. Slightly more copy to maintain than D2a or D2b alone, one page instead of two.
