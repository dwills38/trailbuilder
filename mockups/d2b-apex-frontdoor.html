The flagship's promise holds family-wide: “BuildMyMTB is a free, unbiased bike-building tool with accurate data — pick parts and see, in real time, whether they fit together, plus running price and weight.” Same tool, every ride style.

How D2b coexists with the approved D1 + D4 (it's an addition, not a replacement):
  • With D1 (family switcher): D1 serves people already inside a builder. D2b serves people who aren't in yet — it is one URL you can print on a sticker or say out loud, that answers “which site do I want?” before any product domain is chosen. Every product domain still loads straight to its builder; the front door only exists where there is no builder to gate.
  • Beyond D4 (/home.html): D4 orients people around BuildMyMTB, on BuildMyMTB's domain — a road rider landing there is already in the wrong shop. D2b is the only surface that is bike-type-agnostic before the choice is made.
  • Honest caveat: of this round's variants it overlaps most with what D1's menu already gives — its value hangs on whether a single promotable family URL is wanted. And the only apex currently owned is the company domain (ddubsworks.com), where a pure product-first tone reads slightly odd — see D2c for the merge.
  • Cost: small — one static page plus pointing an apex domain at it.
