How it works

1

Pick parts

Browse the catalog by category — frames, forks, drivetrain, wheels, brakes, cockpit — or search a brand or model.

2

See the fit, live

Every part shows a dot against your current build. Open a part to read exactly why something clashes, in plain mechanic language.

3

Watch price & weight

Running totals as you build. Bundle pricing when a groupset matches. MSRP basis, sale prices always labeled.

4

Share or save

Every build is a link. Share it, compare it, keep it in your garage.

How D3a coexists with the approved D1 + D4 (it's an addition, not a replacement):
  • With D1 (family switcher): D3a lives inside D1 — it adds exactly one item to the approved menu and changes nothing else. The wordmark keeps its current reload behavior; nothing auto-opens this view, ever (the no-popups rule applies to views too — a first-visit auto-home was considered and rejected as an interstitial).
  • Beyond D4 (/home.html): D4 is the full story on a separate page — navigating there mid-build means leaving the app. D3a is the 20-second orientation that keeps your in-progress build loaded, plus a shareable #home deep link; it links onward to D4 for depth rather than repeating it.
  • Cost: medium — a view in index.html's router (the Guides pattern) and ongoing copy maintenance inside the app, vs. zero app changes for the D2 family. The honest question: with D1's menu and D4 shipped, is a third orientation surface earning that?
