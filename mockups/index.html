HOME-PAGE DESIGN ROUND 2 — mockups for Douglas's D2/D3 decision · nothing here ships

Home page — round 2 (the undecided pair)

Where the decision stands: D1 (family switcher) + D4 (/home.html) are approved and building. Still deciding: D2 (a landing at the apex domain) and D3 (a #home view inside the app). Every variant below is an addition to D1+D4, never a replacement — and each states, on-page, what it adds that D4's static page doesn't already give. The round-1 rule stands: no landing page ever appears at “/” in front of a builder.

New this round

D2a — The maker's page at ddubsworks.com

The apex landing as the COMPANY'S page: about-the-builder tone, the products as cards. The canonical face a partner or curious rider finds when they look up who makes these.

Cost: one static page + apex hosting · Gate risk: zero (no builder lives there)

D2b — Apex as the family front door

The apex landing as a product-first front door: “what do you ride?” doors, company as a footnote. One URL to hand a new rider before they know which builder they need. Named neutrally — it assumes whichever family apex domain points at it.

Cost: one static page + apex hosting · Gate risk: zero · Caveat: overlaps D1's menu most

D2c — One apex, both jobs (D2a ⊕ D2b)

The synthesis: doors above the fold, maker's story below — because the only apex currently owned IS the company domain, so both jobs land on the same URL either way.

Cost: one static page + apex hosting · Gate risk: zero

D3a — #home, opened from the D1 switcher

Round 1's D3 re-entered through the approved D1: the #home view's only entry is one new item in the family switcher menu. The wordmark keeps its current reload behavior — round 1's semantics objection, answered.

Cost: medium (a view in index.html's router) · Gate risk: zero — nothing ever auto-opens it

Considered and deliberately NOT mocked: a first-visit auto-shown #home (D3b) — an unsolicited interstitial in all but name, banned by the no-popups rule.

Ranked recommendation, given D1+D4 now exist:
  1. D2c — the apex domain is bought and empty; both apex jobs land on it either way; one small static page, zero gate risk, zero app changes.
  2. D2a — if a single pure tone is preferred: the company face is the one job nothing approved covers; the front-door job is already mostly served by D1's switcher.
  3. D3a — genuinely pleasant (orientation without losing your build) but the third orientation surface after D1's menu and D4's page, at medium cost inside the app. Defer; revisit if D4's About link sees real mid-build use.
  4. D2b — weakest standalone: overlaps D1's menu most, and a pure product-first tone sits oddly on the company domain, the only apex owned.
