HOME-PAGE DESIGN ROUND 1 — mockups for Douglas's pick · nothing here ships

Home page — round 1 (4 directions)

The tension every direction must answer: the site's identity is “loads straight to the data — no pop-ups, no interstitials.” A home page must never become a gate in front of the builder. The one option deliberately NOT offered: putting a landing page at / in front of the MTB builder — that is the betrayal, in any form.

D1 — Switcher, not a page

No home page exists. The header wordmark becomes a click-opened family switcher with the mission line as its footer. The family is one click away from every builder.

Cost: tiny (one menu, per family page) · Gate risk: zero

D2 — Family landing at the apex only

A real landing page — but only where there is no builder to gate (the apex/brand domain, e.g. ddubsworks.com). Every BuildMy domain still loads straight to its builder.

Cost: small static page + apex hosting · Gate risk: zero on product domains

D3 — #home view inside the app

Home is an in-app view (the Guides pattern), opened by clicking the wordmark or sharing a #home link. “/” with no hash is still the builder.

Cost: medium (a view in index.html's router) · Gate risk: zero, but the wordmark's click target changes meaning

D4 — /home.html behind an About link

A standalone static page (the privacy.html pattern) linked from an ℹ️ About button and the footers. The fullest story — mission, provenance, family — zero builder changes.

Cost: small (one page + a deploy.yml cp line) · Gate risk: zero

Directions compose: D1+D4 or D2+D4 are natural pairs. Theme picker bottom-right on every mockup (Loam-first). Mission statement appears verbatim in each: “BuildMyMTB is a free, unbiased bike-building tool with accurate data…”
